3.1 Review the benefits and drawbacks of IT tools and systems used in terms of productivity and efficiency
Could you have been more efficient when making your websOneite? How? Could you have done it in teams?

IT tools can have many benefits in terms of productivity and efficiency, tools like Trello can help reduce time working out what bits of a project have been completed and which still need to be done. Tools like Slack and Microsoft Teams can reduce the number of meetings and conference calls needed on a project and allow team to focus on the creation of content.

In terms of my own project I could have done a better job of planning out the initial layout of my site before starting to put it together, this may have reduced time spent trying out different layouts and struggling with how elements should be positioned.

3.2 Describe ways to improve productivity and efficiency

The best way to improve productivity and efficiency is to reduce the amount of time teams spend away from core tasks. Reducing time spent at and traveling to meetings using Trello, Slack and Microsoft Teams allows teams to still get the key information and focus on the tasks at hand.

Using sites like GitHub to ensure everyone is working on the same codebase also reduces time spent working on conflicting codes due to poor version control. This also allows quick sharing of new ideas and revisions between team members without having to bring the team together for unnecessary meetings and catch ups

Ensuring the scope and plan for the project is correct at the start of a new project will also improve efficiency as the project can be correctly broken down into tasks with out the concern of new tasks having to be added or old tasks being revisited due to a change in requirements.

3.3 Develop solutions to improve own productivity using IT in digital projects
What would you do differently next time? Team work? Use of Slack?

In the future to improve my productivity I would look to use Slack and fellow team mates to help troubleshoot issues and get suggestions on how to solve problems as they arise. I would have also spent more time planning the layout of my site instead of jumping in and trying out different ideas with no initial plan of how the end product would look.

Using Slack and Trello Boards to share the workload with fellow teammates would also free up time for me to focus on other aspects of a specific project and allow me to draw on other team members experience and ideas to improve the site presentation, code and content.

Working with other team members may have also lead to a better design theme and navigation style, whilst also allowing me to add the accessability features I have mentioned previously, such as colourblind modes and audio description tags.

3.4 Describe how you would go about testing digital solutions

When testing digital solutions, it is best to adopt a testing methodology such as Agile or DevOps. This provides a framework for the testers to follow. Testers should embrace the unpredictability that comes with testing within Agile and DevOps teams. Using AI tools, testers can increase test effectiveness, as the tool can identify the areas of code where tests are likely to fail and focus testing on those areas. Getting other team members to test the final product and also testing iterations of parts of the project may also been beneficial to see how they go about interacting with the site and looking for any potential issues end users might encounter. Testing with external users may also be beneficial as they may interact with the project in a different way to technical members of the team. This kind of alpha and beta testing allows you to have many eyes on the project and to comprehensivley test a project. We see this happening in the gaming industry where members of the player base are given access to new features to comprehensivley test them and provide feedback. The more eye on a project the less likely errors or poor design will slip through to the final product

1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Each website should be aware of the source of all its materials and contents and should have the appropriate permissions or licence to use this content. Failure to ensure you have the permission or ownership of the content can lead to potentially costly legal issues, costs associated with redesigning the website or ultimately the website being taken down.

Good examples of these problems can be seen with Youtube and its copyright takedown, copyright strike or content ID claim systems which aim to protect the rights or content creators but also cause issues with false positives and malicious use of these systems

Other issues facing websites is ensuring any content is accurate and fact checked as misleading or false information can lead to reputational damage and legal issues should anyone find issue with the content.

1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account

Describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

Disabled people face many issues when accessing websites dependant on their particular disability, it is our job as website creators to anticipate these issues and design our site accordingly.

For example, people with visual impairment may need to use a screen reader to access a sites content, in this case we should ensure all images have descriptor tags and videos have subtitles and or audio description functions. People with colour blindness may benefit from alternative colour schemes to allow them to better navigate a website and consume its content. Users with hearing impairment may benefit from subtitles for audio and visual content.

1.7 Describe which are the appropriate filetypes for websites

Files come in many types and the appropriate type will depend on the website. Common file types to expect are;

Audio – .wav .mp3 .aac .mid .midi

Video – .webm .flv .mov .qt . avi .rm .wmv .mpg .mpeg .mp4

Images – .jpg .jpeg .tif .tiff .gif .png .svg

Each file type has its advantages and disadvantages and it is dependant on the website creator to select the format which is best for the task. For example, sites with large detailed images may be slow to load on mobile devices and a smaller file size may be desirable at the cost of some quality.

The same applies to video sites like YouTube, storing videos in their raw format can require significant disk space. YouTube compresses the videos uploaded to the site to take up less disk space whilst sacrificing quality. In my personal experience uploading content to YouTube a recording may start life as a multi gigabyte file and after editing and uploading to YouTube the final file may be less than a gigabyte depending on the compression methods used.

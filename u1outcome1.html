1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT in modern business allows companies to offer faster, better and more tailored experiences to their customers, whilst also improving efficiency and driving down costs. A company with a strong IT skill set and backbone is at an advantage compared to a competitor who lacks these skills. IT allows businesses to speed up decision making by providing detailed and accurate information quickly.

IT via digital marketing also allows companies to reach new customers more easily than in the past and information about their customers can be gathered using IT systems such as online browsing habits. Customer service can also be enhanced using IT by allowing customers to easily reach relevant support staff and many problems can be solved remotely without incurring the cost of sending someone to the customers site.

IT also allows business to recruit the best people for the job by removing the barrier of location. Virtual offices can allow staff to work from remote location with teams from around the world

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects
What causes digital projects to fail? Why?

There are many risks which can impact a digital project, some of these can be controlled by the project team and others are due to external factors. Some of the risk are; The initial scope was poorly defined/inaccurate requirements/shifting objectives – This is often an external factor where the client is unsure of exactly what they need or want, however can be caused by poor internal communication about the direction of the project and its requirements.

Trying to do too much work all at once – taking on too much work stretches resources thin and often leads to a shotgun approach where lots of little bits of projects get done but no specific part of the project gets the attention it needs

Disparity of views between Marketing and IT – it is important that from the outset all stakeholders in a project have a clear unified vision of what the outcome of the project should be. Having marketing selling a product that is wildly different to that being developed will lead to unhappy customers and reputational damaged.

Uninvolved project sponsors – again it is vital all stakeholders are on board and clear about the direction of the project, uninvolved participants often surface later in a project only to discover it is not what they expected or required.

Inaccurate estimates and insufficient resources – over committing on a project will lead to short falls in the ultimate output of the project. It is best to be honest about the requirements at the start of a project and plan accordingly.

Each of these ricks can cause potential harm to a project or lead to a product being created that is of no use to the client, as such these issues should be considered when planning out a project.

1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software
How do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

Software and systems should be selected on the basis of what will fit the client needs, are known by your team and will be maintainable going forward, there is no point in writing an application in a dying code which will then need to be rewritten down the line to make it work with more modern systems. Thought should also been given to the roll out of the product, if it is a mobile app on a phone it would potentially be better to write the code in a common language rather than write multiple code bases for the different devices, whereas apps for specific devices might be better written in the devices native language to enable best performance and compatibility with said device.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects
How does GDPR impact digital projects?

All software should be written to comply is local and global laws and guidelines, this often means writing code to defend confidential or sensitive information such as names, addresses, credit card or bank account information. Information should also not be kept longer than it is required, and code should be designed to reflect this requirement.

GDPR meant lots of companies had to go back and ensure their systems were compliant with the news laws and redesign their system if needed. This will have generated a lot of additional work for the systems to be updated to the new requirements and going forward all new system must be designed with these requirements in mind.

If these guidelines are not followed and there is a data breach, then potentially confidential information could fall into the wrong hands causing issues for customers and the company. Such leaks are often featured in the news, examples include the leak for 150,000 confidential NHS records due to a coding error. These breaches are a problem not only the company/organization the caused the leak but also for the subjects of the data leak. This information can be used to exploit people and can cause harassment and harm. The company/organization also opens itself to negative feedback and potentially the loss of business and could also be subject to large fines.

In summary legal guidelines and constraints can cause a large impact on digital projects as they must be considered at all stages of development and future maintenance.
